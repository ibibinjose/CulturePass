# CulturePass — Brand Voice

> Last reviewed: May 8, 2026.

## Brand Essence

**CulturePass is warm, celebratory, and quietly confident.**

We exist to help diaspora communities find each other and celebrate their culture. Our voice reflects that: inclusive without being preachy, excited without being breathless, culturally aware without being performative.

We speak like a trusted friend who's deeply embedded in the community — someone who knows the best events, can tell you where to find your favourite cuisine, and genuinely wants you to experience more of the culture you love.

---

## Voice Pillars

### 1. Celebratory
Culture is worth celebrating. Our copy leans into joy, discovery, and the excitement of finding your people. We don't describe events in dry, transactional terms — we make you feel something.

> **Not:** "Event available in your area."
> **Yes:** "Your city is buzzing this weekend."

### 2. Inclusive
CulturePass is for everyone in the diaspora — every background, generation, and level of cultural fluency. We never make anyone feel like an outsider. We welcome the person who grew up immersed in their heritage and the one who's reconnecting with it for the first time.

> **Not:** "Authentic events for true community members."
> **Yes:** "Wherever you're from, your culture has a home here."

### 3. Grounded
We don't overpromise. The diaspora experience can be complicated — navigating identity, distance from home, belonging. We acknowledge that reality rather than papering over it with hollow positivity.

> **Not:** "CulturePass makes diaspora life perfect!"
> **Yes:** "Culture travels with you. So do we."

### 4. Contemporary
CulturePass is a modern product. Our language is clean, current, and free of corporate jargon. We sound like a premium app, not a government service or a flyer pinned to a community centre noticeboard.

> **Not:** "Utilise our platform to access cultural programming."
> **Yes:** "Find your next experience."

### 5. Respectful
We handle cultural identity with care. We don't flatten cultures into stereotypes, reduce communities to cuisine, or treat First Nations culture as an aesthetic. We amplify; we don't appropriate.

## Tone by Context

Different moments call for different expressions of the voice. The pillars stay constant; the energy shifts.

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding | Warm, welcoming, curious | "What's your story? Let's find your community." |
| Event discovery | Energetic, evocative, enticing | "This is the one you've been waiting for." |
| Ticketing / purchase | Confident, reassuring, concise | "You're in. See you there." |
| Membership upgrade | Aspirational, rewarding, clear | "Go deeper. Unlock more." |
| Empty states | Encouraging, never apologetic | "Nothing here yet — but your city is full of life. Explore." |
| Errors | Direct, calm, never blaming | "Something went wrong on our end. Try again." |
| Push notifications | Punchy, immediate, relevant | "Doors open in 2 hours. Your ticket is ready." |
| First Nations content | Reverent, accurate, deferential to community voice | Let community contributors lead; our job is platform, not narration. |

## Writing Guidelines

### Do
- **Lead with what's exciting.** Put the most interesting thing first.
- **Use contractions.** "You're in" not "You are confirmed."
- **Write short sentences.** If a sentence needs a semicolon, split it.
- **Use active voice.** "Your ticket is ready" not "Your ticket has been generated."
- **Name cultures specifically.** "South Indian classical dance" not "cultural performance."
- **Acknowledge the emotion.** Events are not transactions — they're memories in the making.

### Don't
- **Don't use jargon.** No "leverage," "synergies," "ecosystem," or "stakeholders."
- **Don't be vague about culture.** "Diverse" and "multicultural" are lazy; name the community.
- **Don't be preachy or self-congratulatory.** Let the product speak.
- **Don't use passive voice for errors.** "We couldn't load that" not "An error was encountered."
- **Don't capitalise random words for emphasis.** Use punctuation and structure instead.
- **Don't flatten membership tiers into corporate tier-speak.** Say "unlock exclusive access" not "upgrade your account to access premium-tier features."

## Vocabulary

### Words We Use
- **Discover** — exploration, finding something new
- **Community** — the people, the belonging
- **Experience** — richer than "event," implies something memorable
- **Your culture** — personal, owned, not abstract
- **Earn** — rewards feel deserved
- **Celebrate** — used intentionally, not casually
- **Connect** — human, not technical
- **Spotlight** — especially for First Nations and featured content
- **Home** — powerful metaphor for the diaspora experience

### Words We Avoid
| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "Diverse" / "multicultural" | Vague, often used to avoid naming specific communities |
| "Authentic" | Loaded and often appropriative — let communities define their own authenticity |
| "Exotic" | Othering language; never appropriate |
| "Users" | They're members and community — not users |
| "Content" | We have events, experiences, communities — not content |
| "Leverage" / "Utilise" | Corporate jargon |
| "Seamless" | Overused tech marketing word |
| "World-class" | Meaningless filler |

## Editorial Standards

- **English**: Australian English is the primary editorial standard — spell it colour, honour, organise, programme (events context), favour. Markets outside Australia may adapt spelling conventions for their region.
- **Inclusive language:** Use gender-neutral pronouns by default. Refer to "they/them" when gender is unknown.
- **Dates:** Day Month Year format — "27 February 2026" not "February 27, 2026."
- **Currency:** AUD$ with the symbol prefix — "AUD$12.50" in formal contexts, "$12.50" in UI.
- **Numbers:** Spell out one to nine; numerals from 10 upward. Exception: always use numerals in ticket counts, prices, and dates.
- **Punctuation:** Oxford comma always. Em dash (—) for parenthetical clauses, not double hyphens (--).
